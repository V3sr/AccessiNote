# Product

## Register

product

## Users

AccessiNote is for students, educators, notetakers, and accessibility support teams who need to turn permitted lecture materials into reviewable, source-grounded learning formats. They use it while preparing study notes, checking accessibility coverage, reviewing confusing lecture sections, or auditing whether a recording/transcript contains enough useful context.

## Product Purpose

AccessiNote converts permitted lecture timelines, pasted transcripts, and local video uploads into timestamped study materials. Success means users can quickly see what source material was used, choose an output format for a specific learning need, and export markdown they can review or adapt without relying on cloud services or hidden processing.

## Brand Personality

Careful, capable, and humane. The interface should feel like a quiet working tool: trustworthy enough for accessibility workflows, direct enough for repeated study sessions, and warm enough not to feel clinical.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS landing-page drama, decorative gradients, glassy panels, oversized hero layouts, inaccessible low-contrast grays, and novelty controls. Do not make accessibility support feel like a marketing funnel or a toy; the user is here to get work done.

## Design Principles

- Source trust first: timestamps, OCR, visual descriptions, and warnings should always be easy to find.
- Keep workflows local and explicit: users should understand what the app can and cannot process on this machine.
- Reduce cognitive load: actions should be grouped by task, labels should be plain, and generated outputs should be scannable.
- Accessibility is the product, not an afterthought: keyboard focus, contrast, labels, and motion restraint matter on every screen.
- Polish should clarify state: loading, unavailable tools, missing content, and export actions should be visible without shouting.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast and keyboard-operable workflows. Respect reduced motion, avoid color-only meaning, keep touch targets at least 44px, and make screen-reader notes and source coverage first-class outputs. The product should support users with auditory processing differences, low vision, screen-reader workflows, ADHD, illness-related absences, and language or comprehension barriers.
